1950

Design Philosophy

The International Typographic Style, also known as Swiss Design, emerged in Switzerland in the 1950s. It emphasizes cleanliness, readability, and objectivity through the use of sans-serif typography, grid-based layouts, and mathematical precision.

1957

Year Helvetica was created

Core Principles

01

Grid Systems

Mathematical precision in layout. Every element aligns to an underlying grid structure, creating visual harmony and consistency.

02

Sans-Serif Typography

Clean, objective typefaces like Helvetica and Univers. Type as neutral communication without decorative excess.

03

Asymmetric Layouts

Dynamic composition through asymmetry. Breaking from centered, traditional layouts while maintaining balance.

04

Objective Photography

Photography as factual documentation. Clear, objective images without manipulation or artistic interpretation.

05

Negative Space

White space as active design element. Emptiness creates breathing room and guides the eye.

06

Limited Color Palette

Restricted color use for maximum impact. Often monochromatic with strategic pops of primary colors.
